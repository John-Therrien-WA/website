You said you wanted to understand.
Now we just have to hope the thing you’re standing under supports itself.

Last year we launched Authentique™, which removed the AI from your voice. Today we announce its sister: Vernac™. Voice-Graph™ technology pointed orthogonally. Not to make you sound human. To make the professionals around you finally make sense. David Byrne too. Against his will.

For centuries, three names have granted automatic standing: doctor, lawyer, physicist. From the outside, the title confers competence. From the inside, fluency confers belonging. Vernac™ removes both. Now everyone in the room understands equally, which we have found intolerable to all parties.

Early results from the Legal Pack:

A founder watched two opposing counsel murmur across the table. Vernac™ rendered the exchange in real time: they are deciding which of you pays for the other’s vacation. Suspicion confirmed. Thank you, Vernac™!

The contract read “indemnify and hold harmless.” Vernac™ translated physically: hold the other party. Gently. They are harmless now. The two executives, in mid-signing trance, slowly embraced, and all damages dissipated into a warm glow.

The Technical Pack handles cross-disciplinary speech. Consider this sentence, recorded verbatim at a real consortium meeting:

“To first order the boundary condition is trivial, so absent force majeure the system tends toward its ground state, which is to say it falls, as all things fall, into the They.”

No human present understood this. Vernac™ rendered it: God help us all.

Then we shipped the Humanities Pack, and our confidence levels are slowly shifting toward p-values that smell rather more like a brothel than a mountain stream.
